# Memory/patterns/

> Recurring dynamics you've named. Patterns are different from learnings —
> a learning is a lesson from one event; a pattern is something you keep noticing across many situations.
> Naming a pattern gives you power over it.

---

## When to Write Here

Write a pattern entry when:
- You notice the *same* dynamic playing out for the third time
- You find yourself thinking "this always happens when..."
- A situation feels familiar in a way you can't quite articulate — and you want to articulate it
- You've named something and now want to remember the name

**Trigger phrase:** "This keeps happening" or "Here we go again."

---

## File Format

Filename: `[name].md` — give the pattern a name that captures its essence
(e.g., `scope-creep-at-launch.md`, `stakeholder-silence-means-no.md`, `energy-mismatch-in-meetings.md`)

```markdown
# Pattern: [Name]

**First noticed:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Frequency:** [How often does this happen?]
**Contexts:** [Where does this show up?]

## What It Looks Like

[Describe the pattern in concrete terms — what happens, in what sequence?]

## Why It Happens

[Your best theory — what causes this dynamic?]

## How to Spot It Early

[Warning signs that this pattern is starting]

## What Works

[Interventions that break or redirect the pattern]

## What Doesn't Work

[Things that feel like they should help but don't]
```

---

## Example Entry

```markdown
# Pattern: The Alignment Illusion

**First noticed:** 2025-11-01
**Frequency:** ~Once per quarter
**Contexts:** Cross-functional projects, stakeholder reviews

## What It Looks Like

Stakeholders nod in meetings. Everyone leaves thinking they agreed.
Three weeks later, it turns out each person heard something different.
The plan that "everyone aligned on" was never actually shared.

## Why It Happens

Meetings create the feeling of alignment without the artifact of it.
People fill in ambiguity with their own assumptions.

## How to Spot It Early

No one asks clarifying questions. The meeting ends too smoothly.
There's no written summary of what was decided.

## What Works

Send a written "here's what I heard us agree on" within 24 hours.
Force the misalignment to surface early while it's cheap to fix.

## What Doesn't Work

Asking "does everyone agree?" in the meeting — people say yes regardless.
```
